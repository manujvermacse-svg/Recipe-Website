Butter Chicken

Preparation Time: 45 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Category: Non-Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 800g chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup tomato sauce (pureed)
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 0.5 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 0.5 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken pieces in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, turmeric, and red chili powder for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for best results).
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan or wok on medium-high heat. Once hot, add marinated chicken in batches and cook until lightly golden on the outside (about 6-8 minutes per batch).
  3. Remove cooked chicken from the pan and set aside on a plate.
  4. In the same pan, reduce heat to medium and add 1 tablespoon of butter.
  5. Add finely chopped onions and sauté for 3-4 minutes until soft and translucent.
  6. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  7. Add tomato sauce (or puree) to the pan and stir regularly for about 5 minutes until the oil starts separating from the sauce.
  8. Add remaining butter (2 tablespoons) to the sauce. Stir well.
  9. Return the cooked chicken to the pan and mix thoroughly with the sauce.
  10. Add garam masala, more turmeric if needed, and salt to taste. Stir well.
  11. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing flavors to meld.
  12. Pour in the heavy cream and stir gently until completely incorporated.
  13. Simmer for another 3-4 minutes without boiling.
  14. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, chili powder, or garam masala as needed.
  15. Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with fresh coriander if desired.
