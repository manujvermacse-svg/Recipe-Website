Paneer Tikka Masala

Preparation Time: 50 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Category: Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer cheese (cut into cubes)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 0.5 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 3 tablespoons tomato sauce
  • 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 tablespoons oil or butter
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Cut paneer cheese into 1.5-inch cubes and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, 0.5 teaspoon garam masala, turmeric, and salt to create a marinade.
  3. Add paneer cubes to the marinade and gently mix to coat evenly. Marinate for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan on medium heat. Add cumin seeds, green cardamom, and bay leaf.
  5. When the cumin seeds start crackling (after about 30 seconds), add finely chopped onions.
  6. Fry onions for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown.
  7. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  8. Pour in tomato sauce and cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring continuously until the sauce thickens and oil starts separating.
  9. Add remaining garam masala powder, turmeric, and red chili powder. Mix well.
  10. Slowly add the marinated paneer cubes (along with the marinade) to the pan and gently mix.
  11. Simmer on low heat for 3-4 minutes, allowing flavors to blend.
  12. Add heavy cream or coconut milk slowly and stir gently to combine.
  13. Simmer for another 5-6 minutes without boiling until the sauce reaches desired consistency.
  14. Taste and adjust salt and spices as needed.
  15. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice.
